    Predator prey interactions across scales

    Species interactions are are one of the most basic components of biology. In our biodiverse world all species must make a living by either outcompeting, predating or parasitising other species. These interactions in turn make up the astoundingly complex ecosytems that make up our world.

    Despite the obvious importance of understanding these interactions, the incredible range and complexity underpinning these processes make general predictions hard to pin down. However, within ecology research, approaches have emerged which allow us to make such general predictions based on fundamental aspects of biology such as body size, energy use through metabolic rate and environmental complexity. I am interested in using a variety of approaches to better understand these general patterns with a focus on how intraspecific interactions relate to body size.
